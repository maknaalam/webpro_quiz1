Surabaya Zoo

Surabaya Zoo (Kebun Binatang Surabaya - KBS) is one of the oldest and largest zoos in Southeast Asia, established in 1916. Located in Surabaya, East Java, it is a major local attraction and a center for animal conservation and education, housing a diverse collection of animals, including Indonesian endemic species like the Komodo dragon.

Tunjungan Plaza Mall

Tunjungan Plaza (TP) is the largest and most iconic shopping center in downtown Surabaya. It is a massive, integrated superblock of six interconnected malls (TP 1-6), often cited as one of the largest in Indonesia. It serves as a premier lifestyle and retail destination, featuring a vast array of local and international fashion brands, dining options, entertainment (cinemas, arcades), and is part of a complex that includes hotels and residences.

Ekowisata Mangrove Wonorejo

Wonorejo Mangrove Ecotourism is a protected area on the eastern coast of Surabaya, offering a vital green escape from the city. Visitors can explore the extensive mangrove forest via a boat tour along the river or by walking on bamboo bridges and observation decks. It serves as a natural barrier against erosion and a habitat for various local wildlife, particularly birds and monkeys, offering opportunities for eco-education and conservation.
