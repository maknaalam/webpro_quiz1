Panganan Khas Arek Suroboyoan Iki

Rujak Cingur

Rujak Cingur features a unique mix of sliced fruits and vegetables, rice cake, tofu, and tempeh, all served with a thick, savory, and sweet dark sauce made from fermented shrimp paste (petis), peanuts, and chili. The dish's most distinctive ingredient is the "cingur," which is tenderly cooked cow's snout, adding a chewy texture to the salad.

Semanggi

Semanggi consists of steamed clover leaves (Marsilea crenata) and bean sprouts, topped with a thick, savory-sweet peanut and sweet potato sauce (sometimes containing shrimp paste or petis). It is typically served on a banana leaf "plate" (pincuk) and often eaten with rice crackers (kerupuk puli).

Lontong Balap

Lontong Balap features slices of lontong (compressed rice cake) served with a savory, thin soup containing a generous amount of bean sprouts, fried tofu, and lentho (a fried cake made from mashed black-eyed peas). The dish is usually enhanced with sweet soy sauce and a dollop of spicy shrimp paste (sambal petis), and often paired with mussel satay (sate kerang).
